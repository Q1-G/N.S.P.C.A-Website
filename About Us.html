About Us

Our History, Mission, and Values


Founded in 1995, the National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (NSPCA) has been a dedicated advocate for animal welfare in South Africa. Our mission is clear: to prevent cruelty, promote kindness, and protect animals. Guided by our core values of compassion, integrity, and transparency, we rescue, rehabilitate, and rehome animals in need while actively championing for stronger animal protection laws. Join us in our unwavering commitment to creating a brighter, cruelty-free future for all living beings.


Our Leadership


At the helm of the National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (NSPCA) stands a dedicated and accomplished team of leaders. Annette Rademeyer, our Chairperson, provides strong guidance and vision to our organization, while Brenda Lucas, our Vice Chairperson, offers invaluable support. Marcelle Meredith, our esteemed Executive Director, leads our daily operations with passion and commitment. Together with our esteemed board members, including Vonjia Bauchop, Makhosazane Luthili, Christine Kuch, Chris Marufu, Dudu Moodley, Lize Pienaar, Norma Roberts, and John Penrose, we collectively drive our mission forward and work tirelessly to protect and advocate for animals in need.


Our Achievements


Throughout our history, the National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (NSPCA) has celebrated numerous milestones in the realm of animal welfare. From successful rescue missions that have saved countless lives to legislative victories that have advanced the cause of animal protection, our achievements reflect our unwavering dedication to making a positive impact. Each milestone represents a step closer to our vision of a world where animals are treated with kindness and compassion, and we continue to strive for greater progress each day.
